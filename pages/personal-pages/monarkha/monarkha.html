Yuriy Monarkha, Leading Researcher

monarkha@ilt.kharkov.ua

B. Verkin Institute for Low Temperature Physics & Engineering of Ukrainian Academy of Sciences, 47, Nauky Ave.,
61103 Kharkov, Ukraine

Graduated from Kharkov State University in1972.

PhD Phys & Math, ILTPE, 1977.

DrS Phys & Math, ILTPE, 1988.

Works at the ILTPE since 1974. The co-author of four books. Awards: 1) The medal of National Academy of Sciences of Ukraine (1982), 2) The title of ''Visiting Professor of RIKEN'' under the RIKEN eminent scientist invitation program, Institute of Physical and Chemical Research (RIKEN), Japan (2001).

Main research area:

Quantum transport in low-dimensional electron systems with extremely strong internal interactions, and surface phenomena in quantum liquids.

  • Two-dimensional (2D) Coulomb liquids and the Wigner solid formed on the free surface of liquid helium.
  • Microwave-induced magnetoconductivity oscillations, the negative conductivity effect, and zero resistance states.
  • Quasiparticle reflection from the surface of liquid 3He and its dilute solution in liquid 4He.

Main achivements:

  • The unconventional Hall Effect in 2D electron liquids, and dynamics of highly correlated electrons strongly coupled with medium excitations.
  • Wigner solid mobility on the surface of normal and superfluid 3He.
  • The inter-subband mechanism of microwave-induced conductivity oscillations (MICO); absolute negative conductivity; density domains in a photo-excited 2D electron gas.
  • Anomalous quasiparticle reflection from the surface of a 3He-4He solution.
  • Low frequency edge waves in a strongly correlated 2D electron liquid under a magnetic field.
  • Nonlinear dynamics of a 2D Wigner solid on superfluid 4He.
  • Two-ripplon mechanism of energy relaxation in a 2D electron gas on liquid helium.
